The 12-month arc · goal shifts by phase

Where we focus, and when

At zero subscribers you can't monetize before you have an audience. So the goal evolves: prove acquisition → scale & retain → compound & monetize → self-sustain.

M1WEEKS 1–4
Goal

Prove the acquisition engine

Target: find cost-per-subscriber
Organic
  • Launch daily posting on Instagram + Facebook — the cartoon + one Hot Take, every morning, linking to TMR
  • Post the Hot Takes to X too (text-native, golf-Twitter is active) + free cross-post to Threads
  • Add a referral line to the sweepstakes ("refer a golfer, bonus entry") and "forward to a golf buddy" to the daily AWeber briefing
Paid
  • Run Meta ads (Facebook + Instagram) to the sweepstakes — golf-interest targeting, the Titleist hook
  • Test 3–4 creatives; measure cost per subscriber ruthlessly
  • Keep spend modest while learning what converts — this month is for data, not scale
M3MONTHS 2–3
Goal

Scale winners + prove retention

Target: few thousand engaged
Organic
  • Build the directories into indexed SEO pages ("best golf podcasts 2026", etc.) — compounding traffic
  • Point a few owned domains at TMR for backlinks/traffic
  • Double down on the social content that's pulling
Paid
  • Scale the winning ads from M1 now that cost-per-sub is known
  • Retarget site visitors who didn't subscribe
  • Watch open rates — a list that doesn't open is worthless; retention is the real test
M6MONTHS 4–6
Goal

Compound + early monetization

Target: organic carries growth
Organic
  • SEO pages maturing → steady free traffic; social following established
  • Referral loop compounding — a real share of growth now comes free
  • Pursue the "Advertise" inquiries the site already invites
Paid
  • Test gear-term search ads → Pro Picks pages (affiliate income may offset cost)
  • Sustain sweepstakes acquisition at the proven cost-per-sub
  • First affiliate / ad revenue starts offsetting spend
M12MONTHS 7–12
Goal

Self-sustaining audience + revenue

Target: flywheel + revenue offsets spend
Organic
  • Organic + referral drive most new growth — paid becomes amplification, not the engine
  • TMR is a recognized golf-media destination with a daily-habit audience
  • Direct advertising / sponsorship becomes a real revenue line
Paid
  • Paid spend is optional fuel on proven channels, not survival
  • Affiliate + ad revenue meaningfully offsets — or exceeds — the monthly spend
  • Reinvest profit into scaling what works

The honest money picture

Cashflow over 12 months

Straight talk, because it matters: this is an audience-building investment, not a near-term profit play. The spend goes out first; revenue catches up in the back half. Here's the real shape.

Months 1–3

Investment phase — net negative

Spending to build the audience from zero. Almost no revenue yet (affiliate needs traffic; ads need an audience). Expect roughly −$2–3K/month.

Months 4–6

The bleeding slows

Audience growing — first affiliate commissions (gear clicks) and possibly a first small sponsor start offsetting a portion of the spend. Still net-negative, just less.

Months 7–12

Revenue catches up — target: break-even

If the audience has grown enough, affiliate + advertising revenue meaningfully offsets the spend, approaching break-even. This is the target, not a guarantee — it depends on how big and engaged the list gets.

How we protect the spend

We don't commit the full budget blind. Month 1 proves the cost per subscriber on real data — if it's cheap, the math works and we scale; if it's expensive, we rethink before spending more. Spend is tied to milestones, not a blind 12-month commitment, so the risk stays capped and every dollar is accountable.
